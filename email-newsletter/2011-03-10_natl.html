In The Nation and on NPR: An Investigation of Experimental Prison Isolation Units

Last week, NPR aired 'Guantanamo North': Inside Secretive U.S. Prisons, a segment on its investigation into Communications Management Units (CMUs), prison units designed to isolate and segregate certain prisoners in the federal prison system. The segment featured CCR attorneys Alexis Agathocleous and Rachel Meeropol. And today, The Nation published Gitmo in the Heartland, an article discussing the CMUs and highlighting CCR plaintiffs Jenny Synan and Daniel McGowan.

The majority of CMU prisoners are Muslim or environmental activists and other prisoners with "unpopular" political views. CMU prisoners' right to communicate with their families, friends and the outside world is severely limited, and physical contact with loved ones during their few visits is forbidden. CCR filed a federal lawsuit challenging the unconstitutional nature of the units last year and continues to advocate around the issue. Take action at ccrjustice.org/cmu-action and tell the DOJ to uphold due process and fair treatment.

Victory against Unconstitutional Censorship of Jailhouse Lawyer's Handbook in VA Prisons

The Jailhouse Lawyer's Handbook (JLH) is a legal tool for prisoners to exercise their constitutional rights; learn about relevant law and procedures; and protect themselves from physical abuse, poor conditions and other prison mistreatment. CCR and the National Lawyers' Guild (NLG) publish and provide free copies of the JLH to thousands of prisoners each year. Last year, the Virginia Department of Corrections (VDOC) banned the JLH, claiming that it was a threat to prison security. CCR and NLG filed a suit challenging the ban, and just this month announced a settlement that requires VDOC to remove the JLH from the disapproved publications list; post a notice to this effect in all prisons; allow VA prisoners to receive the JLH in the future without preapproval; maintain copies in VA prison libraries and otherwise make it available to prisoners. Learn more at http://ccrjustice.org/jailhouselaw.

CCR takes the right wing to the Supreme Court in ACORN v. USA

CCR continues to fight Congress' unconstitutional defunding of the Association of Community Organizations for Reform Now (ACORN), a community organization that serves poor, working class people that was targeted by the right wing last year. In March 2010, a federal judge ordered the U.S. government and several federal agencies to rescind orders cutting off funding to ACORN and its affiliates and allies as a result of our lawsuit; however, the Second Circuit panel reversed that decision in August.CCR and ACORN are still fighting for justice and filed a petition for certiorari in the U.S. Supreme Court to have the appeal of ACORN v. USA heard. Learn more about ACORN v. USA.
