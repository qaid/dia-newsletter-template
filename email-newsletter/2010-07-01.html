NYC Premiere! One Night Only - free showing of We Are Not Afraid

One year ago, Honduras experienced a military coup d’etat that unleashed a brutal repression against the Honduran people that is still ongoing today. In the face the assassinations and other rampant human rights violations, an historic resistance movement has risen up throughout the country. Read more in “One Year Later: Honduras Resistance Strong Despite US-Supported Coup” (on the CCR website) by CCR’s Bill Quigley and Laura Raymond.

CCR will be hosting the New York premiere of We Are Not Afraid: Inside the Coup in Honduras, the first feature documentary film about the Honduran Resistance. Please join us in solidarity to shed light on the struggle for democracy and human rights in Honduras.

  • When: Wednesday, July 7, 7pm
  • Where: Tribeca Cinemas (54 Varick Street, New York, NY 10013)
  • What: We Are Not Afraid Premiere and Q&A with filmmaker Katia Lara
  • Price: FREE

For more information: email events@ccrjustice.org

Space is limited so please arrive early.

Contact Assembly Majority Leader Canestrari - Ensure that Senate Bill S-4496 (Anti-Torture Bill) is passed during this session

In a victory for justice, the Gottfried/Duane “Anti-Torture Bill”—that prohibits health care professionals licensed in New York from involvement in torture or the abuse of prisoners, wherever that abuse occurs—has passed the Assembly Rules Committee.

Contact Assembly Majority Leader Canestrari (CanestR@assembly.state.ny.us) and Senator Duane (duane@senate.state.ny.us) and ask them to act quickly to bring S- 4495 to the floor for a vote this week.

Time is of the essence as this legislative session is coming to a close. Take action today to ensure that Senate Bill S-4496 is passed during this session and tell the New Yorkers you know to take action as well.

In April 2009, the Bush administration's "torture memos" and reports by the Senate Armed Services Committee and the International Committee of the Red Cross confirmed that doctors and psychologists were essential to the design, implementation and rationalization of a system of torture and cruel, inhuman and degrading treatment of prisoners in United States custody.

If passed, SB S-4496-A would support health care professionals, protect patients and set a powerful precedent in the fight against torture by removing New York-licensed health care professionals from interrogations and by empowering them to resist unlawful and unethical orders. For more information, visit whenhealersharm.org.

Send a strong message to the New York State legislature, the federal government and the rest of the country that the participation of health care professionals in torture and cruel, inhuman and degrading treatment of prisoners is abhorrent and must be stopped.
