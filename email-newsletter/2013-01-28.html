CCR Sexual Minorities Uganda file federal lawsuit against U.S. evangelical Scott Lively

photo courtesy of photographer name

Last Wednesday, CCR and our client Sexual Minorities Uganda filed a federal lawsuit against U.S. evangelical Scott Lively for persecution against the LGBT community in Uganda. CCR’s complaint detailed Lively’s involvement in anti-gay efforts in Uganda and showed how his actions are aimed at stripping fundamental rights from LGBT persons, in violation of international law. This is the first known Alien Tort Statute (ATS) case seeking accountability for persecution on the basis of sexual orientation and gender identity.

Please see our new webpage about this case and read the New York Times’ coverage of the case for more information about this exciting development.

“U.S evangelical leaders like Scott Lively have actively and intensively worked to eradicate any trace of LGBT advocacy and identity. Particularly damaging has been his claim that children are at risk because of our existence. His influence has been incredibly harmful and destructive for LGBT Ugandans fighting for their rights. We have to stop people like Scott Lively from helping to codify and give legal cover to hatred.”
— Frank Mugisha, Executive Director of Sexual Minorities Uganda

 
 

Civil Rights Organizations Call on FBI to Stop Facilitation of ICE's "Secure Communities" Deportation Program

photo courtesy of photographer name

Last week, CCR joined with the National Day Laborer Organizing Network (NDLON) and over 80 other civil and immigrant rights organizations to send a letter to the FBI demanding that it end its facilitation of ICE’s Secure Communities deportation program.

The Secure Communities program has been advanced despite significant public outcry over its devastating effects on communities, costs to local police, and reports that crime victims fear coming forward due to the program. Moreover the program undermines community policing by turning local police departments into gateways to deportation.

Currently, under Secure Communities, the FBI takes all fingerprints submitted by local police for criminal background checks and automatically forwards the prints to federal immigration officials, regardless of whether individual has been convicted of a crime or of the severity of the charge. The letter recommends that the FBI not forward fingerprints to ICE without the consent of local and state officials.

In 2010, CCR, NDLON and the Kathryn O. Greenberg Immigration Justice Clinic of the Benjamin N. Cardozo School of Law filed a Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) lawsuit which has uncovered thousands of pages of damning documents related to the program's operation.

Learn more about advocacy and litigation efforts around the program at uncoverthetruth.org.

 
 

SNAP Under Attack by Roman Catholic Church

The support group the Survivors Network for Those Abused by Priest (SNAP) has come under increasingly heavy attack by the Roman Catholic Church since September 13, 2011, when CCR filed a complaint on their behalf with the International Criminal Court (ICC). This complaint urges the prosecutor to investigate and prosecute the Pope, the Vatican Secretary of State, and two Cardinals for crimes against humanity for fostering a culture of rape and sexual violence within the Church and enabling, facilitating, and perpetuating the widespread and systemic commission of these crimes around the world.

The Church’s recent attempts to de-legitimize and intimidate SNAP were documented in the March 12, 2012 New York Times article, “Church Puts Legal Pressure on Abuse Victims’ Group,” which includes details on the subpoenaing of the group in two Missouri sex-abuse cases (in which SNAP is not a party) and the efforts to compel them to disclose documents such as confidential communications with survivors. The Times then published an editorial in support of SNAP against these new legal attacks, which they described as “a tactic that could give victims of sexual abuse by priests new reason to retreat into fear and secrecy” (“Hurting Victims’ Advocates”).

Please help stop the attempt to silence SNAP and demand justice and accountability. Learn more about the ICC case.
