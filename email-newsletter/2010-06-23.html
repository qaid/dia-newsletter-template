Demand an Investigation into Illegal CIA Experimentation on People in U.S. Custody

photo courtesy of Mel B.

Strong evidence has emerged showing that CIA medical personnel have been involved in illegally experimenting on people detained in U.S. custody after 9/11. The Center for Constitutional Rights (CCR) has joined Physicians for Human Rights and other human rights organizations to file a complaint to the Office for Human Research Protections, demanding that they investigate evidence of these egregious abuses. The Office for Human Research Protections is part of the Department of Health and Human Services, and has a duty to investigate and ensure accountability for such abuses.

Join us in demanding an investigation into evidence of illegal CIA experimentation on people in U.S. custody by adding your name to this petition

Strong evidence of the CIA’s illegal research and experimentation on individuals emerged earlier this month when Physicians for Human Rights unveiled an extremely significant report called Experiments in Torture: Evidence of Human Subject Research and Experimentation in the “Enhanced” Interrogation Program. This report also shows that the results of the experiments may have been used by attorneys in the Department of Justice to craft a legal framework to shield interrogators from prosecution for torture, and to refine the illegal torture practices used by the U.S. government. The Office for Human Research must investigate this evidence.

NY Supporters: Take a Stand Against Unconstitutional Defunding of ACORN

New York area CCR supporters, please join CCR this Thursday, June 24, 2010 to take a stand against Congress' unconstitutional defunding of the Association of Community Organizations for Reform Now (ACORN).

ACORN, a community organization that serves poor, working class people across the U.S., was the subject of a targeted right-wing attack last year. CCR filed a case challenging Congress' unconstitutional actions, and is heading to court this Thursday to make the message loud and clear: give ACORN the federal funding it is undeniably due.

What:  Oral Argument in ACORN v. U.S.A.
When: Thursday, June 24, 2010 at 10:00 a.m. (please arrive by 9:30 a.m. for security and seating)
Where: United States Court of Appeals for the Second Circuit
500 Pearl Street, 9th Floor
Ceremonial Courtroom, New York, NY 10007
